Camel

  • The ability of the camels to stay for weeks without having to drink water led to them being nicknamed “ships of the desert.”
  • Camels possess either one or two humps that are located on their backs.

Pigeon

  • Pigeons have strong muscles used for flying. They can fly at the altitude of 6000 feet.
  • Pigeons can fly at the speed of 50 to 60 miles per hour.
  • Pigeons can move their wings ten times per second and maintain heartbeats at the rate of 600 times per minute, during the period of 16 hours.

Gorilla

  • Gorillas are primates, an order of mammals that also includes monkeys, apes, tarsiers, lemurs, and humans.
  • There are two gorilla species: Eastern and Western. The scientific name for Eastern Gorillas is Gorilla berinngei, and the scientific name for Western Gorillas is Gorilla gorilla.
  • After chimpanzees and bonobos, gorillas are the closest primate to humans (or any animal, for that matter) in terms of their genetic make up.
